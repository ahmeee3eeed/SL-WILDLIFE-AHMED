Critically Endangered:

  1. Ceylon Arjun Tree (Terminalia arjuna): A tree species facing threats due to habitat loss and over-exploitation.
  2. Sri Lankan Krait (Bungarus ceylonicus): A venomous snake species, threatened by habitat degradation and persecution.

Endangered:

  1. Red Slender Loris (Loris tardigradus): An endemic primate species facing habitat loss and fragmentation, as well as illegal pet trade.
  2. Ceylon Swallowtail (Papilio buddha): A butterfly species endemic to Sri Lanka, threatened due to habitat destruction and fragmentation.
  3. Sri Lankan Frog Species (e.g., Nannophrys marmorata): Several frog species endemic to Sri Lanka are endangered due to habitat loss and climate change.
  4. Ceylon Spurfowl (Galloperdix bicalcarata): An endemic bird species facing threats from habitat destruction and hunting.

Vulnerable:

  1. Sri Lankan Leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya): Although not yet endangered, the Sri Lankan Leopard faces threats such as habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict.
  2. Sri Lankan Elephant (Elephas maximus maximus): The Sri Lankan subspecies of the Asian elephant is considered vulnerable due to habitat fragmentation and human-wildlife conflict.
  3. Sri Lankan Purple-faced Langur (Semnopithecus vetulus): This primate species is vulnerable due to habitat loss and hunting.
